# Broad-Interest and Nonspecialist Lens

## Purpose

Use this lens only when the user asks about a selective general-science venue, broad significance, interdisciplinary readership, or accessibility beyond the immediate methods community.

Keep it subordinate to the statistical review. Broad interest cannot compensate for an unestablished technical case, and technical correctness alone does not establish broad significance.

## Axes

### Originality

Ask whether the main scientific or methodological conclusion is genuinely distinct from close work. When originality is part of the requested assessment, apply [novelty-verification.md](novelty-verification.md). Treat novelty as provisional unless the relevant literature and the manuscript's central citations have been checked.

### Scientific importance

Distinguish:

- a useful technical improvement;
- an important advance within one specialty;
- a result that changes understanding or capability across fields.

Ask what becomes possible, knowable, or interpretable because of the result. Avoid equating benchmark gains with outstanding importance.

### Interdisciplinary interest

Identify who outside the immediate subfield could use or learn from the result and why. Require a concrete connection through a shared scientific problem, transferable principle, or consequential capability.

Do not broaden the audience by listing fields without showing the connection.

### Immediate and longer-term implications

Separate implications demonstrated by the manuscript from plausible future influence. A potentially far-reaching technique is not yet a demonstrated broad scientific result.

### Readability for nonspecialists

Check whether a scientifically trained nonspecialist can understand:

1. the problem;
2. why existing approaches are inadequate;
3. the central conceptual move;
4. the main result;
5. how the result changes the field;
6. the principal boundary.

Do not demand removal of necessary mathematics. Recommend a preflight explanation, schematic, diagnostic example, or clearer summary when it would expose the mechanism without weakening precision.

## Output

Add a concise section to the statistical review:

- broad-interest case currently established;
- likely interested readership and why;
- significance or transferability gap;
- nonspecialist readability barrier;
- one or two highest-value framing improvements.

Do not present this section as an editor's final decision. If venue-specific criteria have not been supplied or verified, label the assessment as a general broad-interest lens.
