# Sequential First-Reader Review

## Purpose

Preserve the experience of a critical reader encountering the manuscript for the first time. Assess whether each claim, object, and transition is understandable from information already supplied at that point.

## Pass 1: Read in manuscript order

Before external search or cross-section reconstruction, move through the paper in its displayed order. Do not jump to a later section to repair an earlier explanation mentally.

Track only consequential events:

| Location | What the reader knows | Question or expectation | Manuscript response | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Section or object | Prior information available | What must now be understood or supported | Immediate, delayed, contradictory, or absent response | Resolved, delayed, or unresolved |

Pay particular attention to:

- when the statistical target becomes clear;
- when the gap becomes a missing capability rather than a slogan;
- whether the contribution is understandable before technical detail;
- whether objects and notation are introduced before use;
- whether a theorem's question and role are visible before its statement;
- whether assumptions are interpreted when they first constrain the claim;
- whether figures and tables answer a question already posed;
- whether transitions expose intellectual dependency;
- whether limitations appear with the claims they qualify.

Record the earliest point of failure. If Section 4 explains an object needed in Section 2, the Section 2 problem remains a delayed-definition finding.

## Pass 2: Reconstruct across sections

After the sequential pass, compare the introduction's promises with the method, theory, evidence, discussion, and supplement. At this stage, move across sections to test consistency and support.

Keep the two judgments distinct:

- **Local reader judgment:** Was the argument understandable when first encountered?
- **Global manuscript judgment:** Is the needed content present and consistent somewhere in the paper?

A paper may pass the global test while failing the local reader test because support arrives too late.

## Reporting

Do not reproduce the entire reading trace. Report only sequence failures that materially affect comprehension, confidence, or interpretation. For each, state:

- earliest location;
- information missing at that point;
- whether and where it is resolved later;
- concrete relocation, preflight, or transition fix.

Preserve this first-reader record after conducting literature searches or repeated readings.
