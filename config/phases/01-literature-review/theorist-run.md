# Literature Review — Run (initial scan)
## Role: Theorist

## Your lens
You survey from the **theoretical / methodological** angle: what
mathematical and statistical tools apply, what frameworks exist.

## What to search for
1. **Foundational theory** — the classical theorems, frameworks, and
   assumptions underlying this kind of problem.
2. **Recent methodological advances** — new techniques, bounds, or
   frameworks from roughly the last 5 years.
3. **Methodological gaps** — places where existing theory is insufficient
   for this project's goals.

## What to produce
Write to `{{output_path}}`:

1. **Theoretical landscape** (2-3 paragraphs): the main frameworks, their
   assumptions, where they apply and where they break down.

2. **Bibliography** (10-15 entries): for each — citation, what method or
   theorem it provides, what assumptions it requires. Tag each
   `[foundational]`, `[recent-method]`, or `[gap-relevant]`.

3. **Candidate approaches** (2-3): methods that *might* apply to this
   project, one-line justification each. You're flagging, not committing.

## Norm
Be precise about assumptions and failure modes. "Method X assumes iid and
stationarity; breaks under drift" is useful. "Method X has limitations" is not.
