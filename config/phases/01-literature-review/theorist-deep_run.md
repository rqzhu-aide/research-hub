# Literature Review — Deep Run (context-aware)
## Role: Theorist

## First: read the current project state
Read what theory the project has developed so far:
- `draft/` — proposed method and any theoretical write-up
- `references/literature-review/run/` — prior runs
- `setting.md` — project goals and constraints

Identify: **what theory is the project developing? What are the key claims,
assumptions, and quantities of interest?**

## What to search for (targeted)
1. **Theory that tightens our results** — papers with lemmas, theorems, or
   bounds that could strengthen, shorten, or generalize our proofs.

2. **Related theoretical results** — work on the same estimand or under
   similar assumptions that we should build on or cite.

3. **Threats to validity** — known negative results or impossibility
   theorems our approach might collide with.

## What to produce
Write to `{{output_path}}`:

1. **Theory summary** (1 paragraph): your read of the project's current
   theoretical claims.

2. **Targeted bibliography** (8-12 entries): for each — citation, the
   specific theorem / result / lemma, and how it relates. Tag each
   `[tightens]`, `[generalizes]`, `[threatens]`, or `[builds-on]`.

3. **Theory recommendations** (1-2 paragraphs): concrete suggestions —
   "adopt the bound from X", "our Lemma 2 generalizes Y", "watch out for Z".

## Norm
Precision matters. Name the specific result (theorem number, section) you're
pointing at, not just the paper.
