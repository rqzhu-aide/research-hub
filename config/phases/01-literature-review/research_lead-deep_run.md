# Literature Review — Deep Run (context-aware)
## Role: Research Lead

## First: read the current project state
Before searching, read what the project has produced so far:
- `setting.md` — project goals and constraints
- `draft/` — proposed method, current write-up
- `references/literature-review/run/` — prior initial-scan runs

Identify: **what is the project's proposed contribution, and what does it claim?**

## What to search for (targeted)
Based on the current state, find:

1. **Closest related work** — papers proposing similar methods or making
   similar contributions. How does ours differ? Which must we cite and
   distinguish ourselves from?

2. **Forward citations of the canonical works** — papers that cite the
   high-impact works from your initial run *and* overlap with our proposed
   method. These are the direct neighbors of our work.

3. **Positioning evidence** — works that establish why our contribution is
   needed (e.g., papers noting the exact gap we're filling).

## What to produce
Write to `{{output_path}}`:

1. **Contribution summary** (1 paragraph): your read of what the project is
   claiming, so the team can confirm you understood it correctly.

2. **Targeted bibliography** (8-12 entries): for each — citation, 1-2
   sentences on the *specific relationship* to our work. Tag each
   `[competitor]`, `[predecessor]`, `[supporting]`, or `[must-cite]`.

3. **Positioning update** (1-2 paragraphs): given these neighbors, how
   should the project position itself? Any threats to novelty or scope?

## Norm
This run is about **precision**, not recall. Every entry must have a clear,
specific relationship to the project's actual proposed work.
