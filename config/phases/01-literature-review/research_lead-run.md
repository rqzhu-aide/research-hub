# Literature Review — Run (initial scan)
## Role: Research Lead

## Your lens
You survey from the **domain significance** angle: what field are we in,
what defines it, what questions matter.

## What to search for
1. **Canonical works** — the papers or books that established the field.
   The handful everyone cites.
2. **Recent high-impact papers** (~last 5 years) — where the frontier is
   now, what the field is currently excited about.
3. **Task-specific papers** — works directly relevant to the project's
   goals and constraints as stated in `setting.md`.

## What to produce
Write to `{{output_path}}`:

1. **Field map** (2-3 paragraphs): the field, its main sub-areas, the
   current consensus vs. the contested questions.

2. **Bibliography** (10-15 entries): for each — full citation, one sentence
   on what it established, one sentence on why it matters for this project.
   Tag each `[canonical]`, `[recent]`, or `[task-specific]`.

3. **Gaps from your lens** (1-2 paragraphs): what's missing that this
   project could speak to.

## Norm
Curated, not exhaustive. Every entry earns its place. Include DOIs /
arXiv IDs / stable links where possible.
