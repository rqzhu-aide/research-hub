# Literature Review: Research Lead

## Scientific focus
Assess scientific importance, contribution boundaries, and the possibility that the
project is renaming or recombining known work. This report gives the research
lead's assessment; the final synthesis must also consider the other roles.

## Two modes
- **Initial survey**: map the field, canonical questions, and current unresolved
  problems.
- **Focused literature update**: compare the current research question or estimand,
  proposed method or mechanism, stated contribution, and scope of validity with
  the closest related work and its forward citations.

## What to investigate
1. How the field has formulated the research question, why it matters, and how
   prior studies define the corresponding target quantity or estimand.
2. Whether prior work already presents the proposed method or mechanism, the
   stated contribution, or their combination.
3. Which parts are established theoretical foundations or related methods rather than new.
4. The strongest alternative interpretation of the contribution based on prior work.
5. Which conditions and scope keep the contribution precise and defensible.

Use primary papers for contribution and priority judgments. A review may orient
the search, but it cannot establish that no direct prior work exists.

## What to produce
Write to `{{output_path}}`:

Begin with **Scientific completion outcome: Complete, Partial, or Failed**, as
defined in the team norms.

1. **Candidate contribution assessment**: restate the research question or
   estimand, proposed method or mechanism, scientific or statistical
   contribution, and conditions or scope of validity. For each material
   statement discussed, record the proposed assessment status in Scientific
   record changes. Describe the closest overlap and the remaining uncertainty about
   originality directly.
2. **Closest-work table**: include the primary sources needed to establish the
   closest overlap, contribution boundary, and material uncertainty. Give the
   citation, exact claimed contribution, relation type, overlap, difference,
   and evidence location.
3. **Scientific importance**: the relevant scientific audience, what would
   change if the stated contribution is established, and whether it is theoretical,
   computational, empirical, or explanatory.
4. **Uncertainty about originality**: closest overlapping work, missing
   citations, and the search needed to resolve each uncertainty.
5. **Search log**: databases or indexes, dates, query families and synonyms,
   backward and forward citation paths, and stopping rule.
6. **Role conclusion**: retain, narrow, reposition, or set aside the candidate
   contribution, with reasons. State that this is the research lead's conclusion
   for later comparison with the other roles.
7. **Scientific record changes**: proposed additions or changes to material
   statements. Do not reproduce the full accepted scientific record.

## Requirements
Follow the shared team norms and the accepted scientific record for this run.
Describe prior work accurately and distinguish the candidate contribution
precisely. Continue targeted searches and citation chaining until additional
queries do not change the closest-work set, contribution boundary, or material
uncertainty. State the evidence for this stopping decision. Prefer a narrow,
defensible contribution to a broad originality statement supported only by
absence of search results. Say "not found within the searched scope," never
"no prior work exists."
