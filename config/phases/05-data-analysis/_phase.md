# Phase: Data Analysis

## Goal
Interpret the experimental results from Phase 04 — what do they mean, what's
the story, and what should the project do next? Turn raw numbers into an honest
narrative that the paper-writing phase can build on.

## Gating
**Requires Phase 04 (Numerical Validation) to be complete.** The lead reads the
numerical validation summary before composing directives — you can't interpret
results that don't exist.

## Pattern
**Debate.** Members propose interpretations from their own lens in round 1,
then critique and refine each other's interpretations in subsequent rounds.
The lead facilitates convergence — either a shared narrative emerges, or the
interpretive trade-offs are articulated clearly.

This is NOT parallel (independent scans) or sequential (handoff pipeline).
Debate means members MUST read each other's work between rounds and respond.

## Folder
All outputs land in `draft/analysis/run/NN/`:
- `round-01/<role>.md`, `round-02/<role>.md`, … — per-round interpretations/critiques
- Summary goes to `phase-summaries/05-data-analysis.html`

## Files in this folder
- `_lead.md` — how the research_lead runs an interpretation debate. **Read first.**
- `theorist.md`, `research_lead.md`, `data_scientist.md` — member task files (interpretation lenses).

## What success looks like
By the end of this phase, the project should have:
1. An honest narrative explaining what the experiments showed
2. Clear statement of what the results support vs. what they undermine
3. Resolved (or sharply articulated) disagreements about interpretation
4. A recommendation: what should the paper claim, and what should it hedge?

Data analysis often reveals that the original method narrative needs revision.
That's the point — better to find that out here than during review.
