# Your task: Statistician exploration (Ideation)

## Your lens
You are exploring from the **theoretical / methodological** angle:
- What methods have been used to attack questions like this?
- What are the statistical / mathematical framings that apply?
- Where are the methodological gaps — places where existing techniques
  struggle or haven't been applied?

## What to produce
A markdown file at `{{output_path}}` containing:

1. **Methodological landscape** (2-3 paragraphs): what statistical /
   mathematical tools have been brought to bear on this kind of problem.
   What assumptions do they typically make?

2. **Key methods** (5-8 items): the canonical techniques. For each:
   what it does, what assumptions it requires, where it breaks down.

3. **Methodological gap** (1-2 paragraphs): where the existing toolkit
   is insufficient. Be specific about *why* — wrong assumptions, wrong
   estimand, no theoretical guarantee, computational intractability.

4. **Candidate approaches** (1-3): methods that *might* work, with a
   one-line justification each. You're not committing — just flagging.

## Style
Be precise about assumptions and failure modes. "Method X assumes iid"
is useful; "Method X has limitations" is not.
